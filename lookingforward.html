Looking Forward

Three goals for the future.

Next semester, I will take two additional data science classes to build my coding and website building skills. I also plan to declare the Data Science and Analytics minor, so my coursework in future semesters will continue to reflect my growing interest in data science.

I hope to dedicate time to reading recently published nonfiction in the realm of digital humanities. Some reads I hope to tackle could include Johanna Drucker’s Graphesis: Visual Forms of Knowledge Production and Yuval Harari’s Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks from the Stone Age to AI. These works will provide valuable insights into the analytical and creative possibilities of digital tools in storytelling and historical writing.

I will continue to attend workshops offered at the EC, Kislak Center, or other library locations around campus. This class introduced me to the myriad opportunities for creative and educational growth that workshops at these locations offer. I hope to attend the Coffee with a Codex workshop that I attended for the Penn Workshop assignment again in the future, and I would love to try more of the creative workshops at the EC, like making vinyl decals.
